Modern Day Presidential Tweeting

About This Site

On July 1st, 2017, the 45th president of the United States of America made a bold claim.

His critics claimed that his stream-of-consciousness, spontaneous and often angry tweeting habit did not have the gravitas appropriate for official communication to the nation from a standing president. In other words, the tweets were "unpresidential". Donald J Trump responded that not only were they not presidential, but were instead "MODERN DAY PRESIDENTIAL".

While the debate continues about what is or is not appropriate in a public official's Twitter feed, many of us are still seeking to understand what world we live in now. To that end, this website is here to help.

Modern Day Presidential Tweeting hosts a database of "presidential" tweets from the current administration (@realDonaldTrump, @POTUS) and the previous one (@BarackObama, @POTUS44), and related retweets. Using the site's interface you can compare for yourself and figure out what the "modern day" in "modern day presidential" means.
